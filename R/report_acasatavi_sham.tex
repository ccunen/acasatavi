\section{Introduction}\label{introduction}

The ACASA-TAVI study is \st{a prospective investigation of losartan as
an adjunctive therapy for patients with glioblastoma}. According to
Protocol \st{Version 7.0 (17-Dec-2024), the trial collects comprehensive
demographic, clinical, imaging, and quality-of-life information prior to
treatment initiation}.

This report summarises \st{the currently available baseline data with
emphasis on clinical status, imaging assessments, and prior treatments
that may influence subsequent outcomes}.

The safety analysis is based on the Safety Analysis Set including all
subjects with any safety information after baseline (N=53). Patients
randomised to hydroxychloroquine without receiving any amount of the
treatment will be excluded from the safety population (nobody was
excluded based on that condition). The primary efficacy analysis is
based on the Full Analysis Set (FAS) including all randomised subjects
who have had at least one baseline and one post-randomisation evaluation
of efficacy (N=51). The secondary efficacy analysis is based on the Per
Protocol Set including all participants of the FAS who took at least 7
of the 8 first hydroxychloroquine doses (N=49) Analysis according to
final SAP version 2.1, dated 28.05.2020 Data extraction Viedoc
electronic data capture system, time stamped: 26.05.2020 12:06 UTC

The aim of this document is to produce tables and figures to be included
into the article as requested by email from Merete Hetland 10 December
2019. The analyses are prespecified in the Statistical Analysis Plan
(SAP) dated 2nd of September 2019, and the locked database with an
update received on the 10th of December dated 26th of October 2019.

\subsection{Randomisation Status}\label{randomisation-status}

The present dataset is built on \emph{sham randomisation} generated from
the most recent export from Viedoc. No true treatment assignments have
been unlocked. All findings in this document should therefore be
interpreted as preliminary and descriptive only. Final efficacy
comparisons will not be conducted until the database is locked and
genuine randomisation codes are used.

\subsection{Data preparations}\label{data-preparations}

The data have been further prepared in accordance with the SAP, except
for the imputation of missing baseline Body Mass Index (BMI) for two
trial subjects. They have been imputed with the median BMI value among
all other ITT subjects. This imputation is not described in the SAP.

\subsection{Specifications to the
analyses}\label{specifications-to-the-analyses}

For the continuous CRP and ESR parameters, I have fitted a generalized
linear mixed gamma model due to skewness. This model gave the best fit
according to Akaike's Information Criterion over a standard linear mixed
model and a generalized linear mixed lognormal model. For the joint
count variables I fitted a generalised linear mixed negative binomial
model over a corresponding poisson model due to overdispersion. All
these specification are according to the analysis plan.
